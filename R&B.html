Rhythm and Blues/R&B

Rhythm and Blues, often abreviated as R&B, originated in African-American communities in the 1940s. The themes of R&B often derived from experiences of African-American experiences of pain and trials experienced throughout time. It encapsulated the quest for freedom and joy through modern and past experiences or ideas, and triumphs and failures in regard of relationships, economics, and aspirations. The usual components of R&B are piano, bass, guitars, drums, saxophones, and sometimes background vocals. In the 1950's, it contributed to the development of rock and roll, then becoming refered to as music styles that would form and incorporate gospel, electric blues, and even soul music! From the 1960s to the 1970s, R&B bands had been introduced more commonly and contemporary R&B was slowly being introduced. As the 1980s had arrived, R&B was more apparent, combining itself with elements of soul, funk, pop, disco, electronic music, and hip hop. The following are famous R&B artists:

Ray Charles

Marvin Gaye

Chaka Khan
