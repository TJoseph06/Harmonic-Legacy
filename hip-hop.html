Hip-hop

In the early 1970s, the Bronx, New York had brought to life a cultural movement in the form of music called hip-hop. It combines various elements of rap, DJing, breakdancing, and graffiti art. For African American and Latinx groups, this was a form of expression to face the social and economic challenges within their communities. Hip-hop was more of a culture than just the music, but the music was a key component of the movement that of which gained mainstream popularity within the 1980s. It had become a global phenominon through artists like LL Cool J, Run-DMC, and Public Enemy. Within the 90s, it diversified into subgenres like gangsta rap, concious rap, and alternative hip-hop.
